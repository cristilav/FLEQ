What is FLEQ?

FLEQ is a system that allows to organizes quizbowl tournaments easily. Its name is an acronym of Free LibreSoft Educational Quizbowl:

  • Free: we use libre software to develop it. You can also download it, install it yourself, modify it and redistribute your modifications.
  • LibreSoft: this project is developed by the LibreSoft Group at the Rey Juan Carlos University in Madrid, Spain.
  • Educational: This game has been developed oriented to educational use.
  • Quizbowl: The type of game we play: it is a championship of quizzes.

The main objective of FLEQ is offer a system that allows to almost autonomously manage tournaments. This autonomy will offer administrators the possibility to organize quizbowl tournaments with hundreds of players and hundreds of different questions in a few minutes, not having to care about infrastructure and other issues.

The FLEQ's Trinity

FLEQ system is divided in three diferent parts. All of them work in harmony to offer the services that administrators need to organize and that players require to play a tournament.

  1. Website: if you're reading this words, you're in it! This part offers the interface to admin and play quizbowl tournaments and all the info about the project to unregistered users.
    • Documentation about the operation of the system.
    • Step by step guides to know how to use all parts of FLEQ.
    • A webclient to play immediatly!
    • Latest news about FLEQ project.
    • FAQ with answers to the frequently asked questions.

  2. Tournament manager: offers different services through its web interface such as:
    • Registration.
    • Question management.
    • Creation new quizbowl tournaments.
    • Participation in quizbowl tournaments.
    • Detailled information about tournaments and games.
    • Selection of the hour and date of the match with your opponents.
    • Revision of question by players and organizers.

  3. FLEQbot: an IRC bot that, inside an IRC channel can execute automatic actions such as:
    • Setting permissions on the users of the canal.
    • Inviting players of a game to play.
    • Posting the questions to be answered by participants.
    • Providing clues about the correct answer to a question.
    • Checking if the answers of the players are correct.
    • Awarding points to the player who correctly answers first a question.
    • Declaring the winner of the match.
