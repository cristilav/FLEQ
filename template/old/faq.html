Administrator privileges are given to users upon request. If you want to obtain admin privileges to create your own quizbowl tournament, request them writing an e-mail and you can organize your own tournaments free.

What happens if I upload to the system more than once the same question?

FLEQ checks if a question with the same text is saved more than once. If this happens, FLEQ discards this question and will continue uploading and saving the rest.

After having chosen my preferred hours to play a game, the game settings don't change. Why?

Date and time preferences by players to a game are only confirmed and changed if both players agree at least in one of them.

For example, if the default game start time is 10 PM and a player chooses 10 AM and 4 PM as his prefferences, the game start time won't be changed until his opponent selects his prefferences times to 10 AM, 4 PM or both. If both players agree on two or more preferences, FLEQ will select by default the earliest time and will modify the start of the game to 10 AM in our case.

What is Freenode?

Freenode, formerly known as Open Projects Network, is an IRC network used to discuss peer-directed and open source projects. You can find channels in different languages of free software projects. You can also find channels of other projects like government organizations and non-government organizations.

The IRC main server is irc.freenode.net; you can read more about Freenode at http://freenode.net.

What is Freenode Webchat?

Webchat or really qwebirc software, is a fast, easy to use and free software IRC client.
Webchat provides a web interface allowing users to access the Freenode IRC network by simply using a web browser.

Is there a different way of accessing to IRC of Freenode Webchat?

Of course! Freenode Webchat is a quick way to access Freenode network from any browser without having to install or configure any software. The main way to access, mostly used by users, is to access with an IRC client installed in your PC, in Freenode or other server.

  • Colloquy, Mac OS X.

How do I configure my IRC client to automatically connect to Freenode?

This setting is focused on getting the IRC client to automatically connect Freenode network and authenticate with your nickname using the password obtained when you registered your account.

Here are the steps for configuring the clients recommended in the preceding paragraph: Xchat, Pchat and Colloquy.

Xchat

  • Through the Xchat menu bar access to Xchat > Network List...
  • In the window that lists the available IRC networks fill the field Nick name with your nick/username. Choose FreeNode in the network list and click Edit.


  • In the configuration window of Freenode network below Connecting select checkbox Auto connect to this network at startup. After that, fill the field Nickserv password with the password obtained when you registered your account.


  • Configuration setting has finished. Now, when you open Xchat, it'll automatically connect to Freenode network and it'll authenticate your nickname/username.

Pchat

  • Through the Pchat menu bar access to Pchat > Network List...
  • In the window that lists the available IRC networks fill the field Nick name with your nick/username. Choose FreeNode in the network list and click Edit.


  • In the configuration window of Freenode network below Connecting select checkbox Auto connect to this network at startup. After thar, fill the field Nickserv password with the password obtained when you registered your account.


  • Configuration setting has finished. Now, when you open Pchat, it'll automatically connect to Freenode network and it'll authenticate your nickname/username.

Colloquy

  • After running the program, in the main window of connections, click the button New.


  • In the configuration window of new connection, click in Details, and fill the field Nickname with your nickname/username. Write the Freenode direction (irc.freenode.net) in the field Chat Server. Check the box Remember this connection and write your password in the field Server Password.


  • After that click in Connect button, and Colloquy will be connected to Freenode network and ready to access to IRC channels.


  • Configuration setting has finished. Now, when you open Colloquy, it'll automatically connect to Freenode network and it'll authenticate your nickname/username.

My answer was written sooner than the other player but he won the point. Why?

Sometimes during the game you will see that even though you sent the correct answer to a question before the other player, it is the other palyer who is awarded the point.

Actually, that's not entirely true. What is happening is that your IRC client shows in your screen the answer you just sent, although it has not arrived at that point at the FLEQbot server, which is the one in charge of evaluating if the answer is correct or not. It may well have happened that in the meantime the answer of you opponent had reached the server and won the point.

In the figure below you will see an example of how player 2, even if he/she thinks that his/her answer was first one (because it appears on the screen of his client first), does not get the point because the response of player 1 had arrived before at the IRC server.



When you are playing a game, try your connection is not too busy sending data by other programs (downloading files, streaming videos ...). In this way, your answers will arrive as quickly as possible to the IRC server and avoid problems like the one shown in the figure.
